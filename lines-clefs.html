Lines

There are a total of 5 lines. In music we use the lines and spaces to identify which notes to play. There are7 notes which include A, B, C, D, E, F ,G the order will change depending on which clef you're using.

In percussion and sometimes in band you'll see just one line instead of 5. This is just for counting rhythms and doesn't have specific notes. Each note has a value depending on what the time signature is. You'll learn more about this in the Time signature lesson!

Treble

The treble clef order starts on the bottom line with E then the next line is G, then B, D, F. The best way to remember this is through the acronyms Every Good Boy Deserves Fudge . The lines that aren't on the lines starting after E is F. The best way to remember this is through the acronym FACE

Instruments that use Treble

High pitch instrements, piano

Bass

The middle line that's between the 2 dots is the note F. The best way to remember this is that the bass cleff is in the key of F hence the way the bass cleff symbol is. After that it starts the normal order G, A, B, C, etc. on the lines, then repeats.

The best way to remember the lines starting from the bottom is the acronym Great Big Dogs Fight Animals. The best way to remember the spaces is the acronym All Cows Eat Grass.

Instruments that use Bass

Lower pitch horns, strings, and pianos
